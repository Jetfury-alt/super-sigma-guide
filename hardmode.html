Hardmode Ores

After entering Hardmode, head over to the crimson and destroy 3 altars using your newly obtained Pwnhammer.

Every time you destroy an altar, a new Hardmode ore gets generated in your world. Each ore will either be one of two from three tiers:

  • Tier 1: Cobalt or Palladium. Spawns in the upper 3rd of the underground and mined with a Molten pickaxe
  • Tier 2: Mythril or Orichalcum. Spawns in the middle 3rd of the underground and mined with a cobalt or palladium pickaxe
  • Tier 3: Adamantite or Titanium. Spawns in the bottom 3rd of the undeground but not in the Underworld and mined with a mythril or orichalcum pickaxe

This will be a very tedious task to do, so drink a spelunker potion:

You would want to collect 45 Cobalt/Palladium ore, exact enough to craft you a Pickaxe to mine the next tier.

Using your Cobalt/Palladium Pickaxe, mine 112 Mythril or Orichalcum ore to craft a Pickaxe and an anvil to have Hardmode items.

For tier 3, you would mine 246 Adamantite ore or 266 Titanium ore.

With your hellforge and 30 of that ore, craft yourself a Titanium/Adamantite orge to convert that ore and make a full set of armour.

Gearing Up

Next thing to do is to upgrade your weapons, accessories and even get yourself a pair of wings.

When Hardmode has been initiated, a new biome called the Hallow will spawn as well as more of your Crimson. These hardmode biome will the generated in the shape of a "V" throughout your entire world, usually the right being the Hallow and the left being the Crimson.

Head over to yuour Drimson infected Desert, and kill the Crimson infected mummies (blood mummies) as they have a 10% chance to drop a Dark Shard.

You would want to go to your underground Crimson biome in the caverns layer, where every enemies (not just native to the crimson) will have a chance to drop a Soul of Night. Continue gathering until you have 10 souls of night.

Now with your dark shards and souls of night, purchase a Shotgun from the Arms Dealer in order to craft the Onyx Blaster at your Mythril or Orichalcum Anvil.

To get yourself some wings, go to your surface Hallow biome and kill pixies, these drop Pixie Dust and you would need to collect 99 of them.

Supa fire tip from Lionel: You might as well try to exlpore your underground Hallow, killing skeleton archers as you go by. These rarely drop the Magic Quiver accessory, allowing you to save 20% of your arrows, a must-have for boss fights and sustainability.

Next, head over to your sky islands by using a gravitation potion or otherwise. At this height, there is a chance for a wyvern to spawn.

If you want to imporve your chances, take a battle potion:

Wyverns are quite tough to kill, so try to dodge its movements as best you can. Use campfires and/or heart lantern if needed.

Wyverns drop souls of flight upon death. You'll need 20 of these to craft a pair of wings.

Using your 3 materials, craft your Fairy Wings:

You should start looking into how to gather souls of night and light quickly:

When you go underground in the caverns layer of your rimson and Hallow, open up a big area to allow as many enemies to spawn as possible. Use dynamite to blow the area up quickly.

The Twins

To prepare for this bossfight, upgrade your ranged weapons by craft 15 souls of light into a key of light:

When this key is placed in a chest, upon closing it will spawn in a Hallowed Mimic, a sometimes very difficult enemy (which can spawned naturally in the underground Hallow).

Repeat this process until you obtain the Deadalus Stormbow:

To summon The Twins, craft a Mechanical Eye summon:

When fighting The Twins, aim for Spazmatism (The one that shoot green projectiles) first, as it causes the most damage.

Try to alternate betwee using holy arrows and ichor arrows. Holy arrows do a lot of damage while ichor arrows applies a defense reduction debuff on the bosses, leading to more damage.

Skeletron Prime

To summon Skeletron Prime, craft a Mechanical Skull summon:

Use the Deadalus Stormbow for this fight, as it has a good AOE (area of effect), meaning all his limbs can be hit at once.

This boss moves not as fast, so feel free to fly big circles around him. Watch out for when he throws his limbs at you.

Try to take out the arms that drop lasers and bomb first.

The Destroyer

To summon The Destroyer, craft a Mechanical Worm summon:

The setup for the boss is a bit different. Set up a platform using slid block that is around one screen height above the nearest block below, that way the boss cannot reach you nor can its lasers.

When The Destroyer's segment is damaged, a probe spawns from it. Focus on killing all the probes first, as it can be overwhelming.

This boss fight is pretty easy, so if you're stuggling with the other bosses, kill this one a few times to craft yourself a set of hallowed armour:

Undergound Jungle

Make sure you collect all the bosses' souls after each time, having a soul of sight, fright and might:

Using the 3 souls and hallowed bars, upgrade your Night's Edge to a True Night's Edge and craft a Pickaxe Axe:

Go over to your underground jungle and start mining chlorophyte using your Pickaxe Axe.

Life Fruit also spawn as well, giving you a permanenet upgrade of 5 health until you have 500.

You are also going to see Plantera's Bulbs. These spawn the next boss, so I don't recommend you to do that now.

Also take a look out for the Jungle Temple, which is a massive pyramid structure built with bricks.

Plantera

Upgrading your wings will make this boss fight easier, but all are pretty rare to obtain:

  • Flame Wings: Requires a fire feather, a 1.33% drop from Red Devils in the Underworld
  • Butterfly Wings: Requires butterfly dust, a 50% drop from a rare enemy in the Jungle.
  • Bee Wings: Requires a tattered bee wing, a 0.67% drop from Moss Hornets in the Jungle.

Craft yourself a Chlorophyte Shotbow:

To summon the boss, break a Plantera's Bulb using your pickaxe.

In the first phase, fly in circles around her to dodge her seed projectiles. Holy arrows work best on this boss as she is slow moving.

In the second phase, she doesn't shoot her seed projectiles anymore. She moves very quickly and is much more aggresive. Try to weave around her tentacles as priority.

The Truffle

To gear up for the next boss fight, go down to your Glowing Mushroom Biome and mine plants to collect a few mushroom grass seeds.

Another easier way to get lots of mushroom grass seeds is to set up Dryad living there.

Up at the surface, build a mushroom biome by placing mud to create a perimeter of at least 100 tiles. Then, by using the mushroom grass seeds, plant the entire place or wait for it to spread.

Ensure to build a house, as the Truffle will in, letting you buy an autohammer:

Using 810 glowing mushrooms and 270 chlorophyte ore (54 chlorophyte bars), you can craft enough shroomite bars to craft a full set of Shroomite armour:

Golem & Jungle Temple

When Plantera is defeated, she will drop a Temple key, allowing access to the entrance of the Jungle Temple.

When traversing the Jungle Temple, watch out for LOTS of traps. You can spot them by the pressure plates in the floor, so just mine them out.

To summon the boss, you would want to use a lihzahrd power cell at a Lihzahrd Altar at the end of the Temple. Lihzahrd power cells are obtained from lihzahrd chest or a rare drop from Lihzahrds.

This room was incredibly small, hopefully yours is a lot bigger.

When fighting Golem, move side to side around him, watching out for his fist charging and his lasers.

Around this time, switching classes to Melee would be preferred. You don't need much, just a change of accessories and an armour piece.

Celestial Pillars

After defeating the Lunatic Cultist, it will drop an Ancient Manipulator which is used to make weapons and armour from now on.

There are also 4 Celestial pillars that spawn, with each pillar specific to their own class:

  • Solar Pillar: Melee
  • Vortex Pillar: Ranged
  • Stardust Pillar: Summoner
  • Nebula Pillar: Mage

Take out the pillar that is specific for your class (solar pillar in your case).

To defeat the tower, you would want to kill 100 enemies in the pillar's range. After kill the enemies, the pillar's shield will be lowered, letting you destroy the pillar.

Each pillar will drop its own fragment upon getting destroyed, letting you upgrade healing potions that heal 200 health. These fragments also allow you to craft new weapons, and armour later on.

Use the solar fragments to either craft a Daybreak which is very good on singular targets or a Solar Eruption which is good for crowd control:

When all 4 Celestial pillars are destroyed, the next boss spawns.

Moon Lord

The true test of a Terrarian. The final boss sleeps quiet, until now...

After the 4 Celestial pillars have been destroyed, you have 60 seconds to get ready and buff up.

When Moon Lord spawns, run to the left or fight of your platforms, targeting the eyes on his hands and forehead.

Keep the eye on his forehead in sight. When it opens up, it's going to look at you, shoot 2 beams, and then centers, at which point it fires its Phantasmal Deathray that deals massive damage.

Continue flying on the same side before switching, as the deathray shoots up to whatever side the player was on before. Do those steps until the eye has popped out.

Rather than switching directions, wait until his Phantasmal Deathray to initiate:

Now, it's just a matter repeat the exact same steps before until all the eyes have popped out:

When the eyes have popped out, Moon Lord's core opens. On an arena, the core inconveniently hovers right below, so fly up to expose it.

There will be lots lasers shooting at you, most of them are unavoidable, so just tank the hits.

If you are unfortunate enough to have not beaten Moon Lord on your first try, you can resummon him by fighting Lunatic Cultist again or craft a Celetial Sigil:
